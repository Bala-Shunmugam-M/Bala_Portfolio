Key Findings
Context
VGTPS is an open-cycle gas turbine (OCGT) peaking station operated by TANGEDCO. Located in coastal Tamil Nadu, it faces high ambient temperatures that directly derate output — the core performance challenge studied during training.
Thermodynamic Study
Analysed the Brayton cycle parameters: pressure ratio (10:1–18:1) and firing temperature (980–1350°C). Studied the efficiency-vs-specific-output design map from GE performance data to understand cycle optimisation trade-offs for simple vs combined-cycle modes.
Degradation Analysis
Identified two degradation classes: recoverable (compressor blade fouling — corrected by water washing) and non-recoverable (blade tip clearance growth — only fixed at major overhaul every 24,000 EOH). Cumulative output loss: 2–6% without part replacement.
Improvement Opportunities
Inlet evaporative cooling to recover 4–8% summer output. Steam/water injection for NOx abatement with simultaneous 5% mass-flow power boost. Online compressor washing protocol to recover 1–3% continuously.
Start-Up Sequence
01
Static start: Starting motor cranks compressor-turbine shaft to ignition speed (~20% of rated speed).
02
Fuel admission & ignition: Fuel admitted and spark ignitors fire at ignition speed.
03
Acceleration: Turbine self-sustains combustion and accelerates towards rated speed.
04
Synchronisation: Generator synchronised to grid at rated frequency (50 Hz).
05
Loading: Active power ramped to dispatch level. Entire sequence: 10–20 minutes — the key advantage over steam turbines which require hours.

01
Coastal Tamil Nadu ambient regularly exceeds ISO 15°C baseline by 10–15°C in April–June, causing 5–9% output derating precisely when peak demand is highest — the double-jeopardy paradox for gas turbine peaking plants.
02
Inlet air filtration, essential for coastal salt and dust protection, introduces a persistent pressure drop. A 10 mbar drop alone accounts for 1.42% power loss and 0.45% heat-rate increase — quantified against GE GER-3567H correction curves.
03
Compressor blade fouling is the dominant recoverable degradation mechanism. Online water washing can recover 1–3% continuously; offline mechanical cleaning required for full restoration at each major inspection interval.
04
The HRSG provision at VGTPS represents an untapped opportunity: converting to combined-cycle would raise thermal efficiency from ~35% to ~50–55%, reducing fuel cost per kWh by 25–35%.
05
The 10–20 minute start-to-full-load capability is VGTPS's core strategic asset in a renewable-heavy grid. Gas turbines provide the fast-response reserve and grid inertia that solar and wind cannot supply.
06
NOx diluent injection (water/steam) suppresses flame temperature and reduces NOx, but carries a 1–2% heat-rate penalty per 1% steam/fuel ratio — a compliance vs efficiency trade-off requiring careful calibration.
